> Researched: April 2026 | Sources: Income Tax Department, PIB, ClearTax, KPMG, Upstox, BankBazaar

## 1. The New Income Tax Act, 2025

### Background
The **Income Tax Act, 2025** came into force on **1 April 2026**, replacing the Income Tax Act, 1961 in its entirety. This is the single most significant structural change for FY 2026-27. The Act was drafted to simplify language, reduce redundancy, and modernise tax administration.

### Key Structural Changes

| Aspect | Old Act (1961) | New Act (2025) |
|---|---|---|
| Total Sections | ~298 (complex, dense) | 536 (clearer, reorganised) |
| Rules / Forms | 511 rules, 399 forms | 333 rules, 190 forms |
| Year Concept | "Previous Year" + "Assessment Year" | Single **"Tax Year"** |
| TDS on salary | Sections 192–194T | Consolidated into **Section 392 & 393** |
| Salary TDS Form | Form 24Q | **Form 138** |
| TDS Certificate | Form 16 | **Form 130** |
| Non-deduction declaration | Form 15G / Form 15H (separate) | Merged into **Form 121** |
| Interest TDS Threshold | ₹40,000 | **₹1,00,000** |
| ITR-3 / ITR-4 Deadline | July 31 | **August 31** |
| MAT Rate | 15% (credit carry-forward) | **14% (made final tax)** |

### Transition Rules
- All assessments, appeals, and proceedings under the 1961 Act (for years before 1 April 2026) remain valid.
- For transactions on or after 1 April 2026: deductors must quote new section numbers (e.g., Section 393 instead of Section 194C). Using old section numbers may cause system-level validation errors.
- The "tax year" concept means AY 2027-28 is now simply "Tax Year 2026-27."

## 2. Tax Regimes Overview

### Two Parallel Regimes
India has two income tax regimes operating simultaneously. The **New Regime (Section 115BAC)** is the **default** — taxpayers who do not opt out are automatically taxed under it.

| Feature | New Regime (Default) | Old Regime |
|---|---|---|
| Governing Section | 115BAC (now under new act) | Original IT Act provisions |
| Default? | **Yes** — opt-out required | No — must actively opt in |
| Switching | Every year at ITR filing | Salaried: every year · Business: restricted |
| Standard Deduction | ₹75,000 (salaried) | ₹50,000 (salaried) |
| HRA Exemption | **Not allowed** | Allowed |
| 80C, 80D, etc. | **Not allowed** | Allowed |
| Employer NPS 80CCD(2) | Allowed @ 14% | Allowed @ 10% |
| Rebate 87A | ₹60,000 (income ≤ ₹12L) | ₹12,500 (income ≤ ₹5L) |
| Better for | Low deduction / young earners | High deduction earners (HRA + 80C + 80D + home loan) |

## 3. New Regime Tax Slabs — FY 2026-27

*No changes from FY 2025-26. Budget 2026 confirmed continuation.*

| Income Range | Tax Rate |
|---|---|
| Up to ₹4,00,000 | **0%** |
| ₹4,00,001 – ₹8,00,000 | **5%** |
| ₹8,00,001 – ₹12,00,000 | **10%** |
| ₹12,00,001 – ₹16,00,000 | **15%** |
| ₹16,00,001 – ₹20,00,000 | **20%** |
| ₹20,00,001 – ₹24,00,000 | **25%** |
| Above ₹24,00,000 | **30%** |

### Effective Zero-Tax Thresholds (New Regime)
- **₹12,00,000** taxable income — Section 87A rebate of ₹60,000 covers full tax liability
- **₹12,75,000 gross salary** — Standard deduction of ₹75,000 reduces taxable income to ₹12L

### Tax Computation Verification
For gross salary of ₹12,75,000 (salaried):
```
Gross Salary:               ₹12,75,000
− Standard Deduction:         (₹75,000)
= Taxable Income:           ₹12,00,000

Slab Tax:
  ₹0–4L   @ 0%  =             ₹0
  ₹4L–8L  @ 5%  =        ₹20,000
  ₹8L–12L @ 10% =        ₹40,000
                         ─────────
Basic Tax:                  ₹60,000
− Rebate 87A:              (₹60,000)  [income ≤ ₹12L]
= Tax after rebate:              ₹0
+ Surcharge:                     ₹0
+ Cess (4%):                     ₹0
─────────────────────────────────
Net Tax Payable:                 ₹0  ✓
```

| ₹5,00,001 – ₹10,00,000 | **20%** |

### Senior Citizens (60–79 Years) — Higher Basic Exemption

| Income Range | Tax Rate |
|---|---|
| Up to ₹3,00,000 | 0% |
| ₹3,00,001 – ₹5,00,000 | **5%** |
| ₹5,00,001 – ₹10,00,000 | **20%** |
| Above ₹10,00,000 | **30%** |

### Super Senior Citizens (80+ Years) — Highest Exemption

| Income Range | Tax Rate |
|---|---|
| Up to ₹5,00,000 | **0%** |
| ₹5,00,001 – ₹10,00,000 | **20%** |
| Above ₹10,00,000 | **30%** |

> **Note:** In the New Regime, all age groups use identical slabs — no special treatment for senior/super senior citizens.

### Old Regime Rebate
Section 87A rebate: taxable income ≤ ₹5,00,000 → rebate of ₹12,500. Effectively, tax is ₹0 for income up to ₹5L under old regime.

## 5. Surcharge Rates

Applied on **tax amount** (not income). Triggered when taxable income exceeds ₹50 lakh.

| Taxable Income | New Regime Surcharge | Old Regime Surcharge |
|---|---|---|
| Up to ₹50,00,000 | Nil | Nil |
| ₹50L – ₹1 Crore | **10%** | **10%** |
| ₹1Cr – ₹2 Crore | **15%** | **15%** |
| ₹2Cr – ₹5 Crore | **25%** | **25%** |
| Above ₹5 Crore | **25% (capped)** | **37%** |

> **Key difference:** New Regime surcharge is capped at 25% even above ₹5Cr. Old regime allows 37% — making old regime significantly more expensive for ultra-high-net-worth individuals.

## 6. Health & Education Cess

**4%** on (income tax + surcharge) for all taxpayers regardless of income level.

No threshold, no exemption — applies universally.

## 8. HRA Exemption Formula

HRA Exemption = **Minimum of:**
1. HRA actually received from employer
2. 50% of Basic Salary (Metro cities) / 40% of Basic Salary (Non-metro)
3. Rent paid annually − 10% of Basic Salary

**Metro cities** for 50% rule: Mumbai, Delhi, Chennai, Kolkata

**Example:**
- Basic: ₹6,00,000 | HRA received: ₹2,40,000 | Rent paid: ₹2,16,000 | Metro
- Exemption = Min(₹2,40,000 · ₹3,00,000 · ₹1,56,000) = **₹1,56,000**

## 9. Employer NPS — Section 80CCD(2)

This is unique — it's available in **both** regimes and doesn't count towards the ₹1.5L 80C limit.

| Regime | Maximum Deduction |
|---|---|
| New Regime | **14% of Basic Salary** |
| Old Regime | **10% of Basic Salary** |

This is one of the most tax-efficient investments for salaried employees, especially under the new regime.

## 10. Regime Comparison — Break-Even Analysis

The old regime is better when total deductions exceed a threshold where the tax savings from deductions outweigh the higher basic slab rates.

**Approximate break-even:** If total old-regime deductions exceed **~₹4–4.5 lakh**, the old regime typically wins.

### Common deduction scenarios:
| Scenario | Likely Better Regime |
|---|---|
| Income ≤ ₹12L, no major deductions | **New Regime** (zero tax) |
| Pays rent, claims HRA of ₹1.5L+ | Depends on total |
| Max 80C + 80D + NPS = ₹2.5L | **New Regime** still better for income ≤ ₹15L |
| HRA + 80C + 80D + Home Loan = ₹5L+ | **Old Regime** |
| Income > ₹5Cr | **New Regime** (25% vs 37% surcharge cap) |

## 15. HRA Optimizer — Detailed Research

### The Core Formula (Section 10(13A), Rule 2A)

HRA Exemption = **Minimum of these 3 conditions:**

| Condition | Formula |
|---|---|
| 1 | HRA received from employer |
| 2 | **50%** × Basic Salary (metro) / **40%** × Basic Salary (non-metro) |
| 3 | Rent Paid − 10% × Basic Salary |

> **Salary** for HRA purposes = Basic + Dearness Allowance (DA) + Commission as % of turnover

### The Critical Insight — Reverse Calculation

Most calculators compute "how much HRA exemption you get." This optimizer does the **reverse**: *how much rent should you pay to fully utilize your HRA?*

**Step 1 — Find the ceiling (max achievable exemption):**
```
Max Achievable = Min(Condition 1, Condition 2)
               = Min(HRA received, city% × Basic)
```
This is the absolute ceiling. No matter how much rent you pay, you can never exempt more than this.

**Step 2 — Find the optimal rent:**
To make Condition 3 ≥ ceiling:
```
Rent − 10% × Basic  ≥  Max Achievable
∴  Optimal Annual Rent  =  Max Achievable  +  10% × Basic
   Optimal Monthly Rent =  Optimal Annual Rent ÷ 12
```

**Step 3 — Tax impact:**
Additional tax saving = OldRegimeTax(current rent) − OldRegimeTax(optimal rent)

### Worked Example

| Input | Value |
|---|---|
| Basic Salary | ₹7,00,000/year |
| HRA Received | ₹2,80,000/year |
| City | Bengaluru (now metro — 50%) |

```
Condition 1 = ₹2,80,000
Condition 2 = 50% × ₹7,00,000 = ₹3,50,000
Max Achievable = Min(₹2,80,000, ₹3,50,000) = ₹2,80,000

Optimal Annual Rent = ₹2,80,000 + 10% × ₹7,00,000
                    = ₹2,80,000 + ₹70,000
                    = ₹3,50,000/year = ₹29,167/month

Verify: Condition 3 = ₹3,50,000 − ₹70,000 = ₹2,80,000 ✓
Exemption = Min(₹2,80,000 · ₹3,50,000 · ₹2,80,000) = ₹2,80,000 ✓ (fully utilized)
```

If previously paying ₹20,000/month (₹2,40,000/year):
- Old Condition 3 = ₹2,40,000 − ₹70,000 = ₹1,70,000
- Old Exemption = Min(₹2,80,000, ₹3,50,000, ₹1,70,000) = **₹1,70,000**
- Unclaimed exemption = ₹2,80,000 − ₹1,70,000 = **₹1,10,000**
- Tax saving from paying ₹29,167/mo instead = ₹1,10,000 × 30% × 1.04 = **₹34,320** (30% slab + cess)

### New Metro Cities — FY 2026-27 (Critical Change)

**Before April 2026 (FY 2025-26 and earlier):** Only 4 cities had 50% HRA rate:
- Mumbai, Delhi, Chennai, Kolkata

**From April 1, 2026 (FY 2026-27 onwards):** 8 cities have 50% rate:
- Mumbai, Delhi, Chennai, Kolkata *(original)*
- **Bengaluru, Hyderabad, Pune, Ahmedabad** *(newly added under Income Tax Rules 2026)*

**Impact:** Employees in Bengaluru, Hyderabad, Pune, Ahmedabad get:
- Higher HRA exemption ceiling (50% of Basic vs 40% earlier)
- More room to claim HRA without paying higher rent

### HRA Compliance Rules (FY 2026-27)

| Rule | Threshold | Requirement |
|---|---|---|
| Landlord PAN | Annual rent > ₹1,00,000 | Mandatory PAN collection and submission |
| Landlord relationship | Any HRA claim | Must disclose relationship (new IT Rules 2026) |
| Receipts | Any claim | Monthly rent receipts required |
| Living condition | All cases | Must actually reside in the rented property |

### When HRA Cannot Be Fully Utilized (Permanently Taxable Portion)

Some HRA received from employer may **always remain taxable** — even if you pay unlimited rent:

```
Permanently Taxable HRA = Max(0, HRA Received − city% × Basic)
```

Example: Basic = ₹6,00,000, HRA = ₹4,00,000, Metro (50%):
- City cap = 50% × ₹6,00,000 = ₹3,00,000
- Permanently taxable = ₹4,00,000 − ₹3,00,000 = **₹1,00,000** (always taxed regardless of rent)
- Max you can exempt = ₹3,00,000
